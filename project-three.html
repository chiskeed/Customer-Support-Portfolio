Diagnosing and Resolving a Mobile Storage Issue

Product: Files by Google

Role: Customer Support Specialist

Situation

A mobile user contacted support after repeatedly receiving a “Storage almost full” notification on their phone.

Despite deleting several photos, the warning persisted. When the customer opened Files by Google, the visible files did not appear to justify the amount of storage being used.

The customer was confused and concerned that their phone might stop functioning properly if the issue continued.

Task

My responsibility as the support specialist was to:

  • Identify the root cause of the storage issue
  • Guide the user through a structured troubleshooting process
  • Provide clear instructions that a non-technical user could easily follow
  • Help the user recover storage space without risking important files

Action

To resolve the issue, I followed a structured support process.

  • 1. Established the Customer’s Goal:

    I first clarified the customer’s primary objective, confirming that their goal was to free up storage space on their phone.

    This ensured the troubleshooting process remained focused on the user’s real problem.

  • 2. Collected Diagnostic Information:

    I asked the user to confirm:

    • The type of storage being used (internal storage)
    • The phone’s total storage capacity
    • The amount of storage currently used

    This helped establish that the phone had 32GB total storage with 29GB already consumed.

  • 3. Investigated Storage Distribution:

    Next, I guided the user to review the storage categories within Files by Google to determine which file types were consuming the most space.

    The breakdown revealed:

    • Apps: 11GB
    • Videos: 9.8GB
    • Images: 3.1GB
    • Downloads: 1.2GB

    This immediately highlighted videos as a major contributor to the storage issue.

  • 4. Traced the Root Cause:

    Since the customer mentioned frequently receiving videos through WhatsApp, I directed them to the location where these files are stored within internal storage.

    I provided clear step-by-step navigation instructions to locate WhatsApp media files and identify unnecessary content.

  • 5. Guided the Customer Through the Solution

    The user located hundreds of saved videos within the WhatsApp media folder.

    I guided them to delete unnecessary files, allowing them to safely remove unwanted media without affecting important data.

Result

The customer successfully deleted unnecessary files and freed approximately 5GB of storage space.

As a result:

  • The “Storage almost full” warning stopped appearing
  • The phone regained sufficient available storage
  • The user understood where hidden media files were being stored
  • The customer also gained awareness of how messaging apps can accumulate large media files over time.

I understand your concern, and I want to reassure you that we’ll work through this together step by step. It’s best to focus on the Android folder, since it houses the files that need to be deleted in one place. Just follow the guide below, and everything will be fine:

Steps to Navigate to WhatsApp Media Files:

1. Open the Files by Google app.

2. From the homepage, scroll to the bottom and select Internal storage.

3. You’ll see several folders (usually arranged alphabetically). Locate and click on the Android folder.

4. Inside the Android folder, you’ll find four subfolders: Data, Obb, Obj, Media. Select Media.

5. Within the Media folder, navigate to com.whatsapp.

6. Inside com.whatsapp, open the WhatsApp folder.

7. Then, open the Media folder.

8. Here, you’ll see multiple folders such as WhatsApp Videos, WhatsApp Stickers, WhatsApp Profile Pictures, WhatsApp Images, and more. These represent all the WhatsApp media files taking up space in your internal storage.

9. Select the folder that contains the files you want to delete (e.g., WhatsApp Videos) and remove the unwanted files.

Note: The folder names are standard across most devices, though slight variations may occur depending on the manufacturer or region. Don’t worry if you see minor differences, the structure is generally the same.
